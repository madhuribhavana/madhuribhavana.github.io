Brand communication and Visual Culture.

A peek into my visual design and branding work

Includes branding of Techniche, North Avenue smart corridor of Atlanta and a smart car service.

As a core team member of the annual technical-management festival of IIT Guwahati, Techniche, I was involved in making posters (instructional, informative and publicising), installations, banners and ads required for the branding of the fest.

UDDs are urban areas— corridors, neighborhoods, campuses, or other geographies— that are used as testbeds for a variety of technologies, policies, and programs. North Avenur Smart Corridor is one such UDD in Atlanta, GA. The task at hand was to create an identity system for the smart corridor.

The task was to create a re-create the brand identity for an existing ridesharing service using automated vehicles. The biggest challenge was to rely on the company’s existing identity system and also bringing in a fresh identity. I chose Waymo - a subsidiary of Google. Waymo's brand language is not that of Google but has a material design aspect to it.

What is Waymo known for? Waymo cars will provide in-car entertainment to their users. Waymo uses Google Play apps (music, movies, books, games etc), Youtube and 3D, AR, VR entertainment to its users. Users can connect their devices and feel as if they are using just one more Google app/service. Fun, reliable, accessible efficient are Waymo's core brand characteristics. The aim in branding is to make the communication seemless. Hence, the minimalist design.
